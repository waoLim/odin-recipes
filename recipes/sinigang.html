Sinigang na Baboy (Pork Sinigang Recipe)

A Symphony of Sour and Savory

Sinigang na Baboy is a beloved Filipino sour soup that combines tender pork with vegetables in a tangy tamarind-based broth.

The dish centers around pork (typically pork belly or spare ribs) simmered in a sour broth until tender. The signature sourness comes from tamarind, though modern versions often use convenient sinigang mix packets.

What you'll need

  • 2 lbs. pork belly
  • 40 grams sinigang mix
  • 1 eggplant sliced
  • 8 okras
  • 12 string beans cut in 2-inch pieces
  • 2 long green pepper
  • 1 tomato wedge
  • 1 onion wedged
  • 3 taro roots
  • 3 ounces daikon radish
  • 6 cups water
  • 3 Tablespoons cooking oil
  • Fish sauce and ground black pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Heat oil in a cooking pot. Saut√© onion and half of the tomatoes.

  2. Add the pork once the onion softens. Cook until it turns light brown.

  3. Pour water into the pot. Let boil.

  4. Add daikon radish and taro roots. Cover the pot and continue boiling in low heat until the pork tenderizes completely.

  5. Add sinigang mix and eggplant. Cook for 5 minutes.

  6. Add long green pepper, okra, string beans, and remaining tomato. Cook for 5 to 8 minutes.

  7. Season with fish sauce and ground black pepper.

  8. Add the spinach. Turn the heat off and cover the pot. Keep it covered for 2 minutes before serving. Share and enjoy!
